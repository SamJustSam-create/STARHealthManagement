Section 2 — Intro Strip

What Is Rapid Trauma Training?

Rapid Trauma Training (RTT) is a specialised training approach for high-risk, time-sensitive operational environments. Delivered through reality-based training and grounded in applied trauma care, it develops the capability to deliver life-saving interventions under pressure while maintaining situational awareness. The methodology is informed by recognised international best-practice frameworks.

Section 5 — Training Overview (Accordion)

What the Training Covers

Practical application of tourniquet placement, wound packing, and pressure bandaging to manage life-threatening bleeding in pre-hospital environments, with emphasis on speed and precision under stress.
Protocols for responding to ballistic, edged weapon, and explosive shrapnel injuries, including chest seal application, wound assessment, and casualty prioritisation in active scenarios.
Techniques for maintaining a patent airway in unresponsive or compromised casualties, including positioning, basic adjuncts, iOxygen, and suction, with emphasis on effective execution under field conditions.
Immersive, facilitator-led exercises that replicate real operational environments, requiring rapid assessment, decision-making, and treatment under pressure.
Coordinated response exercises designed to build communication, role clarity, and shared situational awareness across teams, with a focus on improving interagency operability during mass casualty or multi-patient scenarios.
